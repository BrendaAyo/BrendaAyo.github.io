Kriging Analysis for spatio- temporal variation of ground level ozone concentration


Exposure of high concentration of Tropospheric ozone can trigger a variety of health problems including chest pain, coughing, asthma, bronchitis etc. The aim of the project is to estimate the spatial distributions of ozone using geostatistical method (ordinary kriging) for assessing the exposure level of ozone in California. Ozone data was obtained from 181 U.S. EPAâ€™s monitoring stations distributed across the study area from January 2017 to December 2017. The descriptive statistics indicate that the spatial monthly mean of 8-hour ozone concentrations ranged from 0.0220 (December) to 0.0398 (July). It depicts that the monthly mean of daily maximum 8-hour ozone concentrations was relatively low during the winter months (December, January, and February) and the high during the summer months (June, July and August). Kriging maps revealed that the spatial patterns of ozone concentration were not uniform in each month. This may be due to uneven fluctuation in the local climatic conditions from one region to another. The ozone maps indicate that the concentration values found maximum in the south-eastern region of the study area in the months.
