Urban Sprawl of Ibadan, Nigeria


Working alongside Senait Meles (), our final course project in Tracking the Trackers This project assessed land use change and urban sprawl in Ibadan metropolis between the years 1984 to 2018 using Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and Remote Sensing to analyze and interpret the changes for urban growth monitoring and planning. Landsat satellite images were the data used for the study. Landsat images downloaded from the USGS Earth Explorer were digitally pre-processed using ArcMap 10.6. Supervised Classification was performed using Random Forest algorithm leading to the identification of 5 land use classes in the study area which was validated through accuracy assessment in Weka Software with reference to Google Earth imagery.


To show the trend and distribution of land use features in the study area within the period under investigation, Change detection of Urban growth was evaluated by extracting urban from the classified images with the use of raster calculator. Continuous urban growth was depicted i.e. 28% (1984-2000), 43% (2000-2014) and 21% (2014-2018). owever, the forest area coverage continued depleting to almost zero from 1984 to 2018. Findings showed that expansion of urban areas has been continuous between 1984 and 2018 while forest habitat has been depleting. Urban expansion in Ibadan is outwards from Ibadan city.
